Primary Journeys

The MVP should prove three core motions: developers get source-backed help, client admins control their workspace and usage, and the provider can operate a multi-tenant SaaS safely.

1

Client Developer

Employee of a customer software firm using chat for coding and internal knowledge questions.

1

Signs in and enters the correct client workspace.

2

Asks a Python, PHP, .NET, architecture, debugging, testing, or DevOps question.

3

Backend retrieves universal coding knowledge plus private chunks for that tenant only.

4

Hosted LLM streams a code-aware answer with source citations.

5

Tokens are recorded by developer, conversation, model, and tenant.

6

Developer views personal usage and cannot see other users or tenants.

Outcome: trusted coding help without cross-tenant exposure.

2

Client Admin

Engineering manager, tech lead, CTO, or workspace administrator for a client company.

1

Signs in and enters the client workspace.

2

Uploads docs, repository ZIP files, standards, runbooks, and source files.

3

Ingestion stores files, chunks content, creates embeddings, and writes Qdrant/PostgreSQL metadata.

4

Reviews sources and removes stale or incorrect items.

5

Views tenant usage, package limits, and individual user-wise token usage.

6

Uses billing-ready data to understand package fit.

Outcome: client controls private knowledge and team usage.

3

Central SaaS Admin

Internal provider operator supporting the SaaS business across all clients.

1

Signs in to the provider admin console.

2

Views client workspaces separately with tenant ID, package, usage, ingestion, and health status.

3

Identifies accounts near package limits or with unusual usage.

4

Reviews operational metadata, job errors, logs, and billing-ready usage records.

5

Supports failed uploads, blocked ingestion, and package-limit questions.

6

Cannot retrieve another client's private knowledge through normal chat workflows.

Outcome: provider can operate SaaS without breaking tenant boundaries.

4

Support and Billing

Operational users who keep onboarding smooth and billing explainable.

1

Support selects the correct tenant after a client request.

2

Reviews ingestion status, source count, job errors, and log events.

3

Guides client admin to retry upload, delete stale sources, or check limits.

4

Billing reviews package level, tenant tokens, user-wise usage, and overage.

5

Exports usage data for manual invoice or future Stripe reconciliation.

6

Flags unusual usage spikes for support or product review.

Outcome: onboarding and billing remain operationally credible.

Demo and Validation Journeys

Founder/Product OperatorSelects a demo workspace, uploads safe sample code, asks representative questions, shows citations and usage, then turns evidence into roadmap or investor updates.
Investor ReviewerSees login, workspace isolation, upload, chat, citation, usage metering, and admin reporting to validate that this is a SaaS workflow.
Design PartnerTests whether the product answers real onboarding, debugging, architecture, and internal documentation questions from approved customer data.
